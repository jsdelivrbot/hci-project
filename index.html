Observations

Three people sit at the dinner table to eat at the end of the day. While eating, everyone is using their smartphone — they are looking downwards toward their smartphone screen. One person has earphones on while watching a YouTube video. Another is looking at the day's e-mails and planifying their day for tomorrow. The third is reading articles. When asked questions, short and somewhat dismissive answers are given. Sometimes a person interrupts what they are doing to read and answer to a new message (Facebook, Snapchat, SMS). There is no conversation going on at the table, only occasional short questions and answers.

Two people are in the living room. Netflix is running, the dog is sleeping and humans are silent. One is watching Netflix while the other is reading some articles. Both subjects have their phone nearby ready for any alert. The subject reading article (Female) is often interrupted by the multiple group chat that are going on on multiple platforms (Messenger, SMS, Snapchat), those group chat are mostly for social purpose (not work or school related). The other one (Male) is mostly getting emails related to work and answer them right away. Once the individual activities are completed and they get into a suggestion together, the same scenario arise, as soon as they get an alert, they reply. The main difference is that after the reply, they don't stick to their smartphone to pursue other activities, they join back in the conversation.

The Problem

As we observed, people tend to spend a great amount of time using their smartphones for socialization. Activities are varied: texting friends through SMS or iMessage, checking a Facebook feed, organizing a work-related e-mail inbox, or sending ephemeral messages through Snapchat. Even when in groups, with friends or family, while eating or just hanging out, people tend to confine themselves to their smartphones.

Our observations suggest that this is due to the hard-to-resist pull of sheer information and total entertainment. This is in line with a recent study from the McCombs School of Business at The University of Texas at Austin, which reveals that the presence of person's smartphone nearby is enough to distract them and "reduce available cognitive capacity" [1].

The convenience of social interaction through a smartphone is also hard to match. Sending a message is quick and easy, compared to the effort required by a telephone call or a meeting in person. The entertainment value of the message can also be augmented by including a funny picture, a video, or a series of emojis -- things that aren't available to rely on in conventional face-to-face interaction. A side-effect of the greatly facilitated communication brought by technology is that messages tend to be uniform and direct. Laughter is reduced to a few characters and jokes have trendy and easily-replicable formats. Many conversations are also results-focused -- for example, agreeing on quick meet-ups, or dry Slack chats.

With this in mind, our goal is to add some variety to social interaction and encourage face-to-face conversation. Effectively, we want to create a smartphone application that deters users from excessive use of their smartphone for socialization. However, applications to prevent direct smartphone use already exist. Rather, we want to take a fun-centered approach. To do this, the application would suggest an uncommon word, a phrase, a question, a discussion topic, or other such facilitators to use in real-life conversation.

High-Level Design

high_level_design_hci

The scope of the project is solely based on software development and design. Any hardware used is already existant and will be required to run the application (smartphone) and its infrastructure (server).

Custom Components:

Mobile app: The mobile application will be cross-platform (Android and iOS). It will be created using existant frameworks such as ionic, nativescript or meteor.
Local Database: The local database will be used to store local data, such as word usage history or word for offline usage.
Push Notification Service: The push notification service will be used to notify a user about his "Daily Word Suggestion"

Existing Components:

Word Definition API: This external API will be used to retrieve external words, their definition and other specifications, such as frequency of use and familiarity.
Text to Voice API: This external API will be used so that the user can hear the pronunciation of the proposed word.

Optional Components:

DB Server: The database server can be used for multiple purpose and advanced functionalities. For example, to gather data regarding word usage, application usage, etc..
REST API: This API will be custom made using existing frameworks such as the Django REST Framework. It will be used to have access to data stored in the database.

In order to ensure the project feasibility, we have chosen a set of technologies that matches the variety of skills in our team and where every team member will be important to bring the project to an end.

The mobile app development will be ensure by the skills of all team members combined, which already got experience in both Android and iOS. Also, in order to reduce development time and offer an app for both iOS and Android users, an existing cross-platform framework will be leverage instead of developing two separate applications. This framework is based on web technologies, such as Javascript, HTML, CSS, which can be easily used by our team.

The local database portion of it can be easily manage by one of the team member by using a simple file based, embeded database.

The push notification service will be implemented using the tools built-in the cross-platform framework.

The integration with external services will be made simple, because the response is provided in JSON format, by doing a GET request. Again, any of our team member with web skills will be able to perform this.

Also, the cloud component (Database server and REST API on top of it) has been made optional, because they are not necessary for the application to be running and test by human subject, but once we have a first working prototype, we will re-evaluate if it's possible to add it in. Based on the significant skills of the team in SQL, database and API design, implementing this could be challenging, but could be executed with proper time management.

To ensure proper tracking of all items to be completed and their assignments, the GitHub Projects feature will be used. This will allow all developers to be on track and optimize efforts based on our individual skills.

Finally, the main strategy to make this project feasible is to use everyone's skill effectively by using existing and known technologies that accelerate development and by doing proper task management.
